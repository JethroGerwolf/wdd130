Content

Home page

Hopeful Ranch is all done on horseback. We raise longhorns and we have 200 acres of land. We mainly use quarter horses but we also have some Arabs. We enjoy long days in the saddle and camping under the stars when it comes to herding season. We also have a small garden that we take care of in our free time. It is full of potatoes, corn, onions, and tomatoes. This help provides for our needs. We also grow alfalfa in a couple of pastors to help supply the cow in the winter.

Our family has own this ranch for only 9 year and we still have so much to learn.

How we started

How we started

We started in the summer of 2014. The main driving force for this change was I lost my job and have no way to do it. I used to install satellites but the company went under so I looked for something to do and a good friend from church offered that we start ranching. So that is exactly what we did. It was a massive change of life. I am thankful that my friend helped us along the path and now I can't imagine living any other way.

Starting Ranch

Our ranch started out as only 100 areas and 5 horses all quarter horses. From there we went to the auction where with the help of our dear friends we bought 50 head. That was everything we had. We now have 100-125 head a year that we raise a year. We also now have 200 acres.

Resources

Resources for Starting a ranch.

Websites:

USDA New Farmers: The United States Department of Agriculture (USDA) provides a wealth of resources and information for new farmers and ranchers. Visit their website for guidance on agricultural practices, financing, and more. USDA New Farmers

National Cattlemen's Beef Association (NCBA): If you're interested in cattle ranching, the NCBA offers resources, market information, and educational materials. NCBA

eXtension: This collaborative platform offers a wide range of resources, articles, and webinars on various aspects of agriculture and ranching. eXtension

Ranchers.net: An online community and forum where experienced ranchers share their knowledge, offer advice, and discuss various ranching topics. Ranchers.net

American Rancher: A website offering news, articles, and resources related to the ranching industry, including cattle ranching and agriculture. American Rancher

Books:

The Complete Guide to Buying, Owning, and Selling a Ranch

by Peggy Thompson: This comprehensive guide provides insights into the process of purchasing, managing, and selling a ranch.

"Raising Beef Cattle For Dummies"

by Scott Roy and Nikki Roy: A beginner-friendly book that covers the basics of raising beef cattle, including animal care, feeding, and business aspects.

"Ranching Full-Time on 3 Hours a Day"

by Cody Holmes: This book offers practical advice on ranch management and time-saving techniques for aspiring ranchers.

"Storey's Guide to Raising Beef Cattle"

by Heather Smith Thomas: A well-regarded resource for cattle ranchers, covering all aspects of raising beef cattle, from breeding to marketing.
