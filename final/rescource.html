Rescources for starting a ranch

Websites:

1. USDA New Farmers

USDA New Farmers: The United States Department of Agriculture (USDA) provides a wealth of resources and information for new farmers and ranchers. Visit their website for guidance on agricultural practices, financing, and more.

2. NCBA

National Cattlemen's Beef Association (NCBA): If you're interested in cattle ranching, the NCBA offers resources, market information, and educational materials.

3. eXtension

eXtension: This collaborative platform offers a wide range of resources, articles, and webinars on various aspects of agriculture and ranching.

4. Ranchers.net

Ranchers.net: An online community and forum where experienced ranchers share their knowledge, offer advice, and discuss various ranching topics.

5. American Rancher

American Rancher: A website offering news, articles, and resources related to the ranching industry, including cattle ranching and agriculture.

Books:

1. Raising Beef Cattle For Dummies

by Scott Roy and Nikki Roy: A beginner-friendly book that covers the basics of raising beef cattle, including animal care, feeding, and business aspects.

2. Ranching Full-Time on 3 Hours a Day

by Cody Holmes: This book offers practical advice on ranch management and time-saving techniques for aspiring ranchers.

3. Storey's Guide to Raising Beef Cattle

by Heather Smith Thomas: A well-regarded resource for cattle ranchers, covering all aspects of raising beef cattle, from breeding to marketing.

4. The Stockman Grassfarmer" magazine

While not a book, this publication provides valuable insights into sustainable grazing and livestock management practices.

5. Holistic Management: A Commonsense Revolution to Restore Our Environment

by Allan Savory: Although not specific to ranching, this book offers valuable insights into sustainable land management and can be highly relevant to ranching practices.
